about

Hello! I'm a first year PhD student in computer science at UC Berkeley, researching computer security.

I've previously worked with Facebook's places team on deduplication pipelines, Square's Security Engineering team on open sourcing Keywhiz and certstrap, the Intel Science and Technology Center for Secure Computing SCRUB lab to develop next-generation malware detection software and techniques, and as a graduate student instructor for CS61C (twice) and CS162 (here are my ratings).

I'm also a proud member and previously served as President of HKN, the EECS Honor society.

In my spare time I like to travel, listen to music, and play tennis. While I enjoy working with many aspects of the EECS world, my particular interests include security and machine learning.

research

I'm interested in computer security, particularly in constructing secure and large-scale systems for practical applications.

During my undergraduate years, I worked with the SCRUB group at UC Berkeley to apply machine learning techniques to problems in computer security, especially in the domain of malware detection. In particular, we focused on large-scale malware classification using Apache Spark to analyze labels and features of VirusTotal report samples.
